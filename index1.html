About Us

Skardu, located in the Gilgit-Baltistan region of northern Pakistan, is a breathtaking valley surrounded by the towering peaks of the Karakoram Range. Known as the gateway to some of the world’s highest mountains, including K2, Skardu is a paradise for trekkers, mountaineers, and nature lovers. The region is famous for its stunning landscapes, crystal-clear lakes like Shangrila and Sheosar, and historic sites such as Skardu Fort and Shigar Fort. The Indus River flows through the valley, adding to its scenic beauty. Skardu also offers rich Balti culture, warm hospitality, and a unique blend of Tibetan and Pakistani influences, making it a must-visit destination for adventure and cultural enthusiasts.

Cultural Experiences

Skardu offers a deep immersion into the breathtaking beauty and rich cultural heritage of Baltistan. Known as the gateway to some of the world's highest peaks, Skardu is the perfect place to experience the unique traditions and landscapes of northern Pakistan. The Shigar Valley is famous for its historic forts, traditional Balti houses, and warm hospitality. A stroll through its ancient settlements offers a glimpse into the region’s vibrant history and way of life.

Cultural Activities to Try:

  • Balti Cuisine – Savor local dishes like Mamtu (dumplings) and Balay (noodle soup).
  • Traditional Music & Dance – Experience the soulful rhythms of Balti folk performances.
  • Handicrafts & Weaving – Explore the art of making traditional woolen fabrics and pottery.
  • Trekking & Mountaineering – Discover some of the world's highest peaks and scenic trails.
  • Boating on Satpara Lake – Enjoy the serene beauty of Skardu’s famous alpine lake.

Skardu Cuisine:

Skardu is famous for its rich and flavorful cuisine, influenced by the region’s mountainous terrain and Tibetan heritage. The local dishes reflect a blend of Balti traditions and the need for hearty, energy-rich meals suited to the cold climate.

Balti Cuisine Highlights:

Skardu’s Traditional Dishes: Local specialties like Mamtu (steamed dumplings), Prapu (noodle soup), and Balay (wheat pasta with meat broth) offer a taste of authentic mountain cuisine. Organic & Fresh Ingredients: Many dishes use locally sourced barley, apricots, and dairy, ensuring a fresh and natural flavor. Buttered Tea & Apricot Oil: A staple in Balti households, buttered tea provides warmth and energy, while apricot oil is commonly used in cooking for its rich nutritional benefits.
